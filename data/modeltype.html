You have the option of working with a single lung model or a double lung model. In either case, both models represent the whole pulmonary circulation. This is based on model calibration with the following targets:

  • Subject is 175 cm high and weighs 75 kg
  • Lung height is 20 cm with normal lung compliance
  • Measurements are performed at end-expiration (Ppl=-5 and Pal=0 cmH2O)
  • Target PAPm is 15 mmHg at a CO of 6.45 and LAP of 5 mmHg

If you want to calibrate the model to represent a single lung, for the same size subject, you will have to enter a new target PAPm for a given CO and LAP.

You can re-calibrate the model at any time to represent different physiological and/or pathological situations.

To simulate any situation whether normal or pathological in an adult patient, you can just accept the default calibration (which is stored in a database file) or recalibrate the model. The last calibration used is the one that is stored and used with subsequent runs of the model.

The calibration factors can be reset to the default ones at any time from the Calibration window.

Calibration feature is accessible under the "Window" menu option.
